World Happiness Report 2015 - 2019

This interactive slideshow displays the happiness scores vs GDP per capita for the countries reported in the 2015 to 2019 World Happiness Reports. These reports were created by the UN Sustainable Development Solutions Network and also use data from the Gallup World Poll surveys. The report ranks 155 countries by their happiness levels. The happiness scores are derived from a life evaluation question that asks respondents to think of a ladder (AKA the Cantril Ladder), with the best possible life for them being a 10, and the worst possible life being a 0, and they are then asked to rate their own current lives on that 0 to 10 scale. The report also shows data on six factors that may correlate with a countryâ€™s happiness score, these factors being GDP per capita, life expectancy, generosity, social support, freedom, and government corruption. They have no impact on the total score reported for each country, but they may explain why some countries rank higher than others. This visualization focuses on the correlation between happiness score and GDP per capita.
